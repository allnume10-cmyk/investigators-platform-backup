# TIER™ CaseFlow — Investigator User Guide (Step-by-Step)

*Step-by-step instructions for **investigators**. Screenshot placeholders show where to add your own images later (save files under `docs/images/` and update the paths, or paste images into a Word/Google Doc version of this guide).*

## Before you start

- You need your **login email** and **password** from your administrator.
- You only see **cases assigned to you**. If a case is missing, ask an admin to assign it.

## Part A — Sign in and profile

### A1. Open the app and sign in

1. In your browser, go to your **TIER™ CaseFlow** URL (your team will provide it).
2. Enter your **email** and **password**.
3. Click **Log in** (or follow your org’s sign-up flow if you were told to create an account).

4. Wait until the main workspace loads (left **sidebar** + large content area).

1. Look at the **bottom of the left sidebar**.
2. Click **My Profile**.

| 7 | **Report Hub** | Build text/HTML reports for operations or counsel. |
| 8 | **Voucher Hub** | Filter cases by billing / voucher status. |
| 9 | **System Settings** | Your **Command Identity** (from profile), default rate, **Export & backup** (JSON + CSV for your assigned cases and tasks). |

## Part C — Case Files and the case jacket

### C1. Open Case Files

1. In the sidebar, click **Case Files**.
2. Find the matter you need (scroll or use on-screen search/filter if your build includes it).
3. **Click the case row** (or the open control your screen shows) to open the **case jacket**.

> **📷 Screenshot placeholder — Case Files list**  
> *Capture: list with at least one case visible.*  
> *Suggested filename: `images/05-case-files.png`*

---

### C2. Use the case jacket tabs

At the top of the case jacket you should see tabs such as **details**, **logs**, **communication**, **evidence** (names may appear in lowercase on screen).

> **📷 Screenshot placeholder — Case jacket with tabs**  
> *Suggested filename: `images/06-case-jacket-tabs.png`*

#### Tab: **details**

1. Click **details**.
2. Review or edit case fields: parties, attorney, court dates, status, voucher status, notes, etc., **as your permissions allow**.
3. Save using the button or flow your screen shows (e.g. sync / save).

> **📷 Screenshot placeholder — Details tab**  
> *Suggested filename: `images/07-tab-details.png`*

#### Tab: **logs** (activity log)

1. Click **logs**.
2. Add a **new activity** row (or edit existing) with:
   - **Date**
   - **Activity code** (pick the code that matches the work—e.g. interview, court, travel).
   - **Narrative** (what you did—clear enough for an attorney or auditor).
   - **Hours** (billable time if applicable).
3. Save or confirm so the entry is stored.

> **📷 Screenshot placeholder — Logs tab with one activity**  
> *Suggested filename: `images/08-tab-logs.png`*

**Tip:** Log activity **the same day** when possible so vouchers and reports stay accurate.

#### Tab: **communication**

1. Click **communication**.
2. Add a log entry for calls, emails, or notes tied to this case (fields depend on your build).
3. Save.

> **📷 Screenshot placeholder — Communication tab**  
> *Suggested filename: `images/09-tab-communication.png`*

#### Tab: **evidence**

1. Click **evidence**.
2. Add or update rows: what was requested, from whom, dates requested/received, notes.
3. Save.

> **📷 Screenshot placeholder — Evidence tab**  
> *Suggested filename: `images/10-tab-evidence.png`*

4. Close the case jacket when done (use **Close** or the **X**, per your UI).

## Part D — Investigative Tasks

1. Click **Investigative Tasks** in the sidebar.
2. Scan **due dates** and **priority** (e.g. Critical / High / Medium / Low).
3. Open a task (click the row/card) to jump to the related **case** when the app supports it.
4. Mark tasks **complete** when finished (checkbox or control on the task).
5. Some tasks may show **needs intake**—complete missing case/intake information, then clear or finish per your office procedure.

> **📷 Screenshot placeholder — Task list**  
> *Suggested filename: `images/11-investigative-tasks.png`*

## Part E — Communication Hub

1. Click **Communication Hub**.
2. Open an item (email / call / text) from the list.
3. **Match** it to the correct **case** if the app suggests one or lets you choose.
4. Confirm or save so the message is tied to the case for your records.

3. Review **your** cases in that segment.
4. Update **voucher status** or notes **in the case jacket** when your office tells you to, so this hub stays accurate.

## Part I — Report Hub (investigator)

1. Click **Report Hub**.
2. If you see filters (e.g. attorney), set them as instructed by your supervisor.
3. Under **Step 2 — Choose Strategy Protocol**, pick a report, for example:
   - **Weekly Status Report**
   - **Aged Voucher Audit**
   - **Case Aging & Pipeline**
   - **Stagnant Advisory**
   - **Pre-Trial Readiness**
   - **Global Intel Brief**
4. If the screen offers **Output format** (e.g. Plain / HTML), pick one.
5. Run **generate** / execute (button name may vary).
6. Copy or save the output as your office requires.

> **📷 Screenshot placeholder — Report Hub report list**  
> *Suggested filename: `images/16-report-hub.png`*

*Note: Some organizations hide certain reports or add others. If you do not see an option described here, ask your administrator.*

## Part J — System Settings (what investigators usually see)

1. Click **System Settings**.
2. **Export & backup** (at the top) — use these anytime you want a **personal copy** of your work (for your records, transition, or peace of mind):
   - **Download my data (JSON)** — one file with your **assigned** cases, activity logs, communications, evidence rows, and tasks (best for a full backup).
   - **Export my case registry (CSV)** — spreadsheet-style summary of **your** cases (aligned with what you see in **Case Files** / list filters when you export).
   - **Export my tasks (CSV)** — **your** task list in spreadsheet form.
   - If **Excel** puts everything on one row or shifts columns, use **Registry .tsv** / **Tasks .tsv** (tab-separated) instead — same data, usually lines up correctly.
3. **Command Identity** — **Agency name** and **Lead investigator** show what you entered in **My Profile**. To change them, click **Open My Profile**, edit, **Save**.
4. **Default billing rate** — only change if your office told you to; ask if unsure.
5. If your account is **inactive**, you may see a short notice at the top of **Export & backup** — the same download buttons still apply.

## Quick reference — Activity codes

Your app includes many **activity codes** (e.g. **IN** Intake, **SU** Status update, **CR** Court, **TR** Travel). The exact list and labels appear in **System Settings** (if visible to you) or in the **logs** dropdown when you add an activity. Always pick the code that **best matches** the work and write a **clear narrative**.

## If something goes wrong

| Problem | What to try |
|---------|-------------|
| Cannot sign in | Reset password (if offered) or contact your administrator. |
| Case not in list | Ask an admin to **assign** the case to you. |
| Wrong name in sidebar | Open **My Profile** and **Save** again. |
| Report won’t run | Check filters; try again; contact admin if the button stays disabled. |
| How do I back up my data? | **System Settings** → **Export & backup** → use **Download my data (JSON)** and/or the CSV exports (your assigned cases and tasks only). |
