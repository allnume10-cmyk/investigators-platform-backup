# Global Intel Brief – What’s Real Data vs AI-Generated

## Data the app actually sends to the brief

The Global Intel Brief receives **only three numbers** from the app:

| Metric | Source | Definition |
|--------|--------|------------|
| **Active matters** | `activeMattersCount` | Count of cases where **status = Open** (not closed). |
| **Urgent** | `urgentActionCount` | Count of cases that have a **next court date** within 7 days (past or future). Formula: `nextCourtDate` exists and `calculateDaysDiff(nextCourtDate) >= -7`. |
| **Revenue** | `totalSettlement` | Sum of **amount paid** on cases where **voucher status = Paid**. So: total revenue from paid vouchers only. |

The prompt then says something like: *“Virtual Chief of Staff Briefing. Matters: X, Urgent: Y, Revenue: $Z. Format as a strategic report. Use horizontal rules between sections. Today is [date].”*

Everything else in the brief is **generated by the AI** from that short prompt. The model invents section titles, categories, dollar amounts, and narrative to “sound like” a strategic briefing.

## Sections you asked about – where they come from

### High-value surveillance

- **Not** from tasks marked “surveillance” or any task type in the app.
- The app does **not** send task text, task types, or activity codes to the intel brief.
- This section is **AI-generated**. The model may use the word “surveillance” because it fits a generic “investigative firm” template.

### Corporate due diligence

- **Not** from a “corporate due diligence” category in the app.
- The app has no case category or matter-type field sent to the intel brief.
- This section is **AI-generated**.

### “$21K pending billables” (or similar)

- **Not** calculated in the app.
- The app does **not** send:
  - unbilled amounts
  - voucher status counts (Missing, Submitted, etc.)
  - estimated or pending revenue
- So any “pending billables” or “21K” figure is **AI-invented**, not from your data.

### Missing persons / locate orders

- **Not** from case types, task types, or any “missing persons” or “locate” flag in the app.
- The app does not send case categories or matter types to the brief.
- This section is **AI-generated**.

### “Potentially increase this week’s revenue by an additional $2,500” (Financial analysis)

- **Not** from a formula in the app.
- The app does not send:
  - pipeline value
  - “recoverable” or “at-risk” amounts
  - conversion rates or targets
- So that dollar amount and the “potential increase” idea are **AI-invented**, not calculated from your data.

## If you want the brief to be data-driven

To have sections and numbers that **map to real app data**, you’d need to:

1. **Send more structured data** (e.g. counts by voucher status, list of cases with missing vouchers and optional “estimated” or standard billing amounts, task counts or types if you add them, case tags/categories if you add them).
2. **Define each section in code** (e.g. “Pending billables” = cases with Missing voucher × an estimate, or sum of a field; “Surveillance” = tasks or cases tagged surveillance, etc.).
3. **Tighten the prompt** so the AI only summarizes and narrates that data, and does **not** invent dollar amounts or categories you didn’t send.
